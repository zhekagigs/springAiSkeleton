The 2022 Winter Olympics held curling competitions at the Beijing National Aquatics Centre. The competitions, which included men’s, women’s, and mixed doubles, took place every day from February 2 to February 20. This marked the eighth time curling was part of the Olympic program.

Ten nations competed in each event. The mixed doubles competition expanded for its second appearance in the Olympics. A total of 120 quota spots were distributed to the sport of curling, an increase from the 2018 Winter Olympics. Three events were contested: one for men, one for women, and one mixed.

Qualification for the Men’s and Women’s curling tournaments was determined through two methods: placement in the top six at the 2021 World Curling Championships and through Olympic qualification events held in 2021. Six nations qualified via World Championship placement, while three nations qualified through qualification events.

For the mixed doubles competition in 2022, the tournament field expanded from eight to ten. The top seven ranked teams at the 2021 World Mixed Doubles Curling Championship qualified, along with two teams from the Olympic Qualification Event. As the host nation, China automatically qualified teams, resulting in ten teams per event in the curling tournaments.
